Solar System Simulation Game is an educational 2D game where players adjust planetary properties to create habitable conditions. The simulation uses physics-based models to show planetary motion, climate, and habitability. It helps students understand how different factors influence a planet's environment. The game also raises awareness of global climate and environmental issues. Students can learn physics concepts while gaining experience with simulations and programming. The game features a retro, canvas-based arcade style. Technologies used include JavaScript, HTML, and CSS.

At the start of the game, players define the properties of their three planets, adjusting factors such as planetary mass, number of moons, rings, type of planet, and the size of the atmosphere, hydrosphere, and lithosphere. Players also set the properties of the sun, including its type, as well as the number of comets and meteorites in the system. During gameplay, players can control all three planets, switching between them to adjust speed, direction, and rotational velocity. The goal is to maintain planetary conditions that support life: surface temperature, atmosphere, and other factors affect a bioactivity slider, which increases under favorable conditions and decreases under poor ones. The player wins when bioactivity reaches a level capable of supporting a modern civilization.

Technologies used
